Splatoon Amiibo: How the Gear Unlocks Actually Work

Splatoon rewards amiibo more than most franchises

Compared to a lot of Nintendo titles, Splatoon games have historically treated amiibo as a genuine content unlock system rather than a minor bonus. Tapping a Splatoon-line amiibo (Inkling Boy, Inkling Girl, Inkling Squid, and later Octoling variants) typically unlocks single-player challenges tied to that figure.

What you generally get

  • Bonus single-player missions — short, self-contained challenge levels separate from the main campaign.
  • Exclusive gear — completing those challenges usually rewards a piece of clothing or gear you can't get any other way, which is the part that matters most to completionists.
  • Costume changes for your amiibo character — some entries let you dress up the in-game amiibo-summoned character.

Do non-Splatoon amiibo work in Splatoon games?

Splatoon has, at times, supported a wider range of amiibo than just its own line for smaller cosmetic bonuses, but the deepest content (the bonus missions and exclusive gear) is generally reserved for Splatoon-series figures. If gear-hunting is your goal, prioritize the Inkling/Octoling amiibo specifically.

Worth it for a completionist?

If you're chasing 100% gear collection in Splatoon, yes — this is one of the few franchises where amiibo genuinely gate exclusive content rather than just handing out a redundant costume.
