Kirby and Mario Party Amiibo: The Underrated Unlocks Worth Knowing

Kirby amiibo: costumes with a twist

Kirby-line amiibo (Kirby, King Dedede, Meta Knight, and Waddle Dee among them) typically grant Kirby a themed costume ability tied to the character tapped, changing his appearance and sometimes a minor ability flourish while playing as that costume. It's a lighter system than Splatoon's exclusive gear, but it's a nice bonus for series fans specifically.

Mario Party amiibo: minigame-focused bonuses

Mario Party's amiibo support has historically centered on unlocking bonus minigames or a dedicated amiibo party mode/board, rather than granting permanent player advantages. It's more about extra content to play than about giving one player an edge over another, which keeps it friendly for multiplayer game nights.

Why these two lines get overlooked

Neither unlocks anything as visible as Zelda's gear drops or Splatoon's exclusive clothing, so they don't get talked about as much in collector circles. But if you already own either game, the amiibo bonus is a genuinely fun extra rather than a token gesture.

Worth buying for the unlock alone?

Kirby and Mario Party figures are best thought of as "nice bonus if you're buying for the display anyway" rather than "buy specifically for the unlock." The Splatoon and Zelda lines still give more functional value per figure if unlocks are your main motivation.
