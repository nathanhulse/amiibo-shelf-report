Amiibo Cards vs. Amiibo Figures: What's Actually Different

Same NFC tech, very different product

Both amiibo cards and amiibo figures use the same underlying NFC chip technology, but they're built for different purposes in the Animal Crossing ecosystem, and it's easy to buy the wrong one if you don't know the difference.

Amiibo cards

  • Thin, wallet-card sized, sold in blind packs of several cards per pack
  • Used with New Horizons via the in-game NookLink phone app (which requires a smartphone with the Nintendo Switch Online app) or a supported peripheral
  • Let you invite a specific villager to visit your campsite, and eventually to move in if you keep at it
  • Collecting the full card set is its own hobby — there are hundreds of villager cards across multiple series

Amiibo figures

  • Full 3D figures, generally villager or special character models (K.K. Slider, Isabelle, Tom Nook, and others)
  • Tap directly on the console rather than through a phone app
  • Typically used for photo-mode features or specific in-game interactions rather than full move-in invitations
  • Priced and marketed as display pieces first, functional accessory second

Which should you collect?

If your goal is inviting specific villagers into your town, cards are the functional choice and far more affordable per-character. If your goal is a shelf display, figures win on presentation. Plenty of collectors end up doing both for different reasons.
