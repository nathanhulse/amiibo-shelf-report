Zelda Amiibo Explained: What They Actually Unlock in BOTW and TOTK

Zelda amiibo are about gear, not story

If you're hoping an amiibo unlocks new dialogue or a hidden ending, it doesn't. In both Breath of the Wild and Tears of the Kingdom, Zelda-line amiibo are almost entirely about cosmetic and combat gear tied to the character on the figure.

What tapping one generally gives you

  • A gear set drop — many Zelda amiibo grant a themed outfit piece or weapon/shield tied to that character's game of origin (for example, older Link amiibo tend to hand out classic tunics and shields).
  • Once-per-day materials — some amiibo can be tapped roughly once every 24 hours for a small supply drop of in-game materials, which is handy early on but becomes less important once you're established.
  • No permanent stat boosts — nothing from an amiibo makes your save file objectively stronger than a player who skipped them entirely; it's a head start, not an advantage that lasts.

Do BOTW and TOTK amiibo support overlap?

Largely yes — most Zelda-series amiibo that worked in Breath of the Wild continued to function in Tears of the Kingdom, though the specific item granted can differ between the two games even for the same figure.

Are they worth collecting for gameplay alone?

Honestly, they're a nice-to-have rather than a must-have. The real draw for most collectors is displaying the figures themselves — the gameplay bonus is a pleasant extra, not the reason to own one.
