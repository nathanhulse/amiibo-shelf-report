How to Display Your Amiibo Collection Without Wrecking the Boxes

Decide your lane first: boxed or loose

Before buying any shelving or cases, decide whether you're a boxed collector (unopened, mint packaging matters) or a display collector (figures out, boxes recycled or stored). Trying to do both halfway is how boxes get crushed and figures get dusty.

For boxed collectors

  • Keep boxes out of direct sunlight. UV fades cardboard and plastic window inserts surprisingly fast — even indirect window light over months will do it.
  • Store upright, not stacked flat. Stacking boxes horizontally for long periods can warp the box or shift the figure inside against the plastic window.
  • Watch humidity. Cardboard boxes in damp basements or garages will develop soft corners and mildew smell over time; a closet or bedroom shelf is safer than storage units in humid climates.

For out-of-box display

  • Dust is the main enemy. A simple glass-front cabinet cuts down on dusting dramatically versus open shelving.
  • Avoid adhesive figure stands near heat sources — direct sun through a window can soften the plastic base of cheaper display stands over time.
  • Group by series, not release date. Visually, a shelf reads much better organized by franchise (all Zelda together, all Splatoon together) than by when you bought them.

The middle ground: rotate

Many collectors keep a "currently displayed" shelf of favorites and box the rest. It's the most space-efficient option and keeps your display feeling fresh instead of just accumulating clutter.
