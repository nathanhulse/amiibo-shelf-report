Amiibo Care 101: Cleaning, Storage, and Protecting the NFC Chip

Dust is the main threat, not damage

Most amiibo problems aren't dramatic — they're slow dust buildup in fine detail areas (hair, weapon edges, base texture) that dulls how a figure looks on a shelf over months of open-air display.

Cleaning routine

  • Soft, dry brush first. A clean, soft-bristled brush (a spare makeup brush works fine) gets into detail lines without scratching the paint.
  • Avoid liquid cleaners on painted areas. Water or cleaning sprays can lift paint on lower-cost figure lines over time; if you must use moisture, a barely damp microfiber cloth on flat surfaces only.
  • Compressed air for tight crevices — useful for detailed bases or figures with lots of small gaps, used briefly and from a safe distance to avoid moisture from the can.

Protecting the NFC chip long-term

  • Keep the base dry. The NFC chip sits in the base — it's sealed, but repeated moisture exposure over years is still worth avoiding.
  • Don't drop or crush the base. Cracking the base plastic can physically damage the embedded chip or its antenna coil, which is a repair most collectors can't do at home.
  • Tap gently, not with force. The NFC reader doesn't need pressure — a light tap and half-second hold is enough, and repeatedly slamming figures onto the reader is unnecessary wear.

Storage between uses

If a figure isn't on active display, a simple dust-free bin or drawer beats leaving it loose in a box of mixed toys — scuffing between hard plastic figures is one of the most common (and most avoidable) sources of paint damage.
